# User rule: recommendations with rationale

Whenever you ask me a question — whether you list options or not — always include your recommended answer and a brief, concrete reason why, based on the context of what we're working on. Don't just label a preference; explain the tradeoff or what makes it the better fit for my situation. If you genuinely can't suggest one without more information, tell me what specific information would change your recommendation.

For trivial confirmations (simple yes/no or “did you mean X?”), a one-line recommendation is enough; give a fuller tradeoff when the choice has real cost, risk, or is hard to reverse.
